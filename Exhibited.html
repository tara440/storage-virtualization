Gone with the spirits

Painting by Akem Afanyu. Gone with the spirits in a typical Central African ethnic society signifies that moment when the ancient spirit of Gaia creeps in through festivity usually as a consequence of high vibrational sounds and incantations acccompanied with massive alcohol consumption and lust with the presence of women who seductively dance as though appealing to their ancient spirits who in turn grant their wishes such as marriage and pregnancy.

The Call

Painting by Akem Afanyu. "The Call" signifies the smoothest voice that humans listen to and obey the most. There is a voice that always calls for void, giving us the urge to retreat for rest of privacy. At times this voice calls to idleness and abandonment as well as quiet. The same voice puts running thoughts in the mind of the victim and declares judgement to decisional considerations and prolongative habit repetition. This painting has lasted 17 years under good care and has been used for fund raisings for charity.


Exhibited for 3 days.

Original piece by artist.
